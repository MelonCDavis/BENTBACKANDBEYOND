TATTOO AFTERCARE

Your new tattoo isn't just a piece of art. I did the tattoo to the best of my ability, but now it's up to you to take care of it. Here is how to do just that.

Derm Shield / Saniderm

1-WASH YOUR HANDS!

Wash your hands thoroughly with warm water and a liquid antibacterial soap. You need to sing the chorus of the song "Jolene" by Dolly Parton roughly twice to meet the CDC's recommended 20-second handwashing time.

2-REMOVE YOUR BANDAGE

Peel horizontially away from the tattoo. As it stretches the bandage may tear. That's ok. The bandage stretches and the adhesive lets go. Do this in the shower or over a sink due to the fluid that can build up underneath. It can be messy.

3-WASH YOUR TATTOO!

Thoroughly wash your tattoo with hot water and a liquid antibacterial soap. You can use Dial Gold, but please avoid the foam soaps as they have a lot of alcohol in them. Always wash your hands before touching your tattoo until it is fully healed.

4-DRY YOUR TATTOO!

Use a fresh square of paper towel to blot the tattoo dry. Wiping hurts. Don't do it! Please don't just use a hand towel. Used once, a handtowel has enough bacteria to cause an infection in an open wound. Just use the paper towel.

5-YEP ALCOHOL!

I provide alcohol swabs so you can remove any leftover bad guys and residiual soap. This ensures the bandage's success. Do not moisturize the tattoo at this point. The tattoo needs to be dry for the bandage to work properly.

6-PREPARE YOUR BANDAGE!

Make sure the bandage will cover the entire tattoo with at LEAST 1/2 inch border around the edges. Peel the white backing off the bandage being careful to not let it adhere to itself. If it does, you can either do traditional aftercare, or use an alternative derm.

7-PEEL AND REVEAL!

Place the bandage carefully down and press it firmly down working to ensure adhesion. Use the yellow arrows on the top of the bandage as a guide to remove the top plastic. Remember if it leaks that mean things can get out AND in.

8-LEAVE IT ALONE!

Your tattoo is going to look worse before it looks better. it's likely that fluids will build up in the bandage. That's normal and totally expected. Remember that aftercare is not a sprint but a marathon. Your goal is to finish without scabbing!

9-REPEAT 1-4!

In 3-5 days remove the second bandage and repeat steps 1-4. I know you get it. You're an old pro. Healed tattoos a million times. I'm glad you did this one different. I'm proud of you. Different can be weird. Weird can be good!

10-MOISTURIZE!

Your tattoo will likely peel like a sunburn at least one more time before all is said and done. A simple lotion such as Aveeno works well to help keep you moisturized. Avoid petroleum based products as they can clog your pores and lead to a bad time.

11-SUNSCREEN!

So it's your tattoo but remember it's up to you now to keep it looking as good as possible. The sun is not your tattoo's friend. It's job is to fade your tattoo's vibrant colors and make it look old and dingy. Use some sunscreen. Your welcome!

TRADITIONAL AFTERCARE

1-WASH YOUR HANDS!

Wash your hands thoroughly with warm water and liquid anti-bacterial soap. 20 seconds is recommended by the CDC. Always wash your hands before touching your tattoo until it is fully healed.

2-REMOVE YOUR BANDAGE!

Remove the bandage after 2-4 hours. Do NOT re-bandage your tattoo. You've probably heard a million different things from saran wrap to thera-bands. Use the K.I.S.S method. Less really is more.

3-WASH YOUR TATTOO!

Wash your tattoo with hot running water, a mild liquid anti-bacterial soap and a bare hand. Repeat the washing process until the tattoo is free from residue. This may take several rinse and repeats.

4-DRY YOUR TATTOO!

Using a fresh square of papertowel, blot your tattoo dry. Do not wipe it. It is both painful and causes undue irritation. Repeat the previous step if there is still residue at this point.

5-JUST THIS ONCE!

Apply a small amount of Aquaphor Healing Ointment or Hustle Butter to the tattoo until absorbed. Blot any excess off with a fresh paper towel. Excessive amounts of ointment can result in a bad heal.

6-REPEAT & REPEAT!

Repeat the previous steps *excluding step 2* for 2-3 days. When the tattoo begins to show signs of dryness and itching switch to an unscented lotion like Aveeno. STOP USING THE AQUAPHOR!

7-LOTION AS NEEDED!

Repeat the lotion regime for the next two to three weeks or until your tattoo is fully healed. You really can use just about any lotion you want, just avoid fragrances and lotions with alcohol. Read the ingredients!

8-SUNSCREEN!

So it's your tattoo now but remember it's up to you now to keep it looking as good as possible. The sun is not your tattoo's friend. Go outside! Just use some sunscreen. Your welcome!
